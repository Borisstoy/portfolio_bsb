Light Painting

Basics

While discovring photography in New York City back in 2011 and practiced it later on in Barcelona, I experimented light-painting. Using a film camera -the famous Canon AE-1-, I played with long exposure effects.

Exposing to the light

Taken at night or in a dark room, long exposure shots or 'light painting' consist in holding the camera still using a tripod for instance and opening the aperture for a certain period of time, letting the light coming in and 'printing' the film. That's how we get motion effects or duplicate subjects. The longest you expose your film to light, the more 'movement' you can catch.
